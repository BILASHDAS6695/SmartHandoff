# US-002: Display Personalised Discharge Instructions in Patient's Language

> **Epic:** EP-010 — Patient Portal | **Sprint:** 2 | **Points:** 3 | **Priority:** Must Have

## User Story

**As a** discharged patient,
**I want** my discharge instructions displayed in my preferred language with clear sections for medications, activities, and warning signs,
**so that** I can understand my care plan without needing to ask anyone to translate for me.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] **AC-1:** Portal home shows discharge instructions fetched from `GET /portal/instructions` (patient JWT-authenticated); instructions display in patient's `preferred_language` by default; language switcher available for all languages with approved translations
- [ ] **AC-2:** Instructions structured in clearly labelled sections with icons: 💊 Your Medications, 🏃 Activity, 🥗 Diet, 📅 Your Follow-up Appointment, ⚠️ Warning Signs — when to call your doctor
- [ ] **AC-3:** Each medication in "Your Medications" shows: drug name (brand + generic), photo placeholder, dose, frequency, purpose in plain language, and special instructions (e.g., "take with food")
- [ ] **AC-4:** Warning signs section uses large red/amber icons; "Call 911 now" items in bold red; "Call your doctor today" items in amber
- [ ] **AC-5:** Portal accessible on all modern mobile browsers (iOS Safari, Chrome Android) at ≥375px viewport; touch targets ≥44px; confirmed via BrowserStack or Playwright mobile emulation tests

## Definition of Done

- [ ] 5 language variants tested (en, es, fr, zh, pt) in staging portal
- [ ] Playwright mobile emulation tests pass on iPhone 12 and Android viewport sizes
- [ ] Plain-language health literacy review completed by health communication reviewer

## Dependencies

- EP-010 US-001 (Patient authenticated via OTP)
- EP-004 US-002 (Patient instructions generated)
- EP-004 US-003 (Multilingual instructions generated)
