# US-027: Generate Patient-Friendly Instructions in 5 Languages

> **Epic:** EP-004 ‚ÄĒ Documentation Agent | **Sprint:** 2 | **Points:** 5 | **Priority:** Must Have
> **Status:** Draft | **Date:** 2026-07-14

---

## User Story

**As a** patient (Pat) who speaks Spanish,
**I want to** receive my discharge instructions in Spanish, written at a grade-6 reading level,
**So that** I can understand what to do at home and recognise warning signs without needing an interpreter.

---

## Business Value

Joint Commission standard RC.02.04.01 mandates instructions in the patient's preferred language. Without this, non-English-speaking patients face preventable readmissions from misunderstood instructions. Supporting 5 languages (FR-022) expands SmartHandoff's addressable patient population and achieves BO-10 (multilingual support for 5+ languages).

---

## Acceptance Criteria

### Scenario 1: Patient instructions generated at ‚Č§6th-grade reading level (English)
**Given** an English discharge summary has been generated
**When** the patient instructions generator runs
**Then** the instructions have a Flesch-Kincaid Grade score of ‚Č§6.0 (verified via `textstat.flesch_kincaid_grade()`); the output avoids medical jargon and uses plain language.

### Scenario 2: Spanish translation generated with ‚Č§15% semantic delta
**Given** English patient instructions have been generated
**When** Gemini translates to Spanish
**Then** a back-translation quality check is performed; the semantic similarity between original English and back-translated text is ‚Č•85% (cosine similarity of sentence embeddings), confirming translation accuracy.

### Scenario 3: Patient's preferred language is detected and applied automatically
**Given** a patient record has `preferred_language=fr` (French) in their FHIR `Patient.communication`
**When** patient instructions are generated
**Then** the primary output is French; the English version is also stored as a fallback.

### Scenario 4: Unsupported language falls back to English
**Given** a patient's `preferred_language=ja` (Japanese, not in the supported 5)
**When** patient instructions are generated
**Then** English instructions are generated and stored; the `Document.metadata` records `language_fallback=true` and `requested_language=ja`.

## Technical Notes

- `textstat.flesch_kincaid_grade()` returns a float; target ‚Č§6.0
- Simplification retry: if grade >6, re-prompt Gemini: "Rewrite the following at a 6th-grade reading level: {text}"
- Back-translation: `en ‚Üí target ‚Üí en`; use `paraphrase-multilingual-MiniLM-L12-v2` model for cosine similarity
- 5 languages √ó generation cost: use Gemini Flash (not Pro) for translations to reduce cost and latency
