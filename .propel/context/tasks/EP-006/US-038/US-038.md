# US-038: Fire ED Boarding Alert at 2-Hour Threshold

> **Epic:** EP-006 — Bed Management Agent | **Sprint:** 2 | **Points:** 3 | **Priority:** Must Have
> **Status:** Draft | **Date:** 2026-07-14

---

## User Story

**As a** bed manager,
**I want to** receive an automatic alert when a patient in the ED has been waiting for a bed assignment for 2 hours without placement,
**So that** I can escalate the bed search before the patient's wait time impacts clinical outcomes or ED throughput metrics.

---

## Business Value

The Joint Commission tracks ED boarding as a patient safety indicator. A 2-hour unassigned patient in the ED is a boarding event that must be escalated. Automated alerting (FR-043) ensures this is never missed during shift changes or high-census periods, contributing to the 40% ED boarding reduction target (BO-05).

---

## Acceptance Criteria

### Scenario 1: Boarding alert fires exactly at 2-hour mark
**Given** an A01 admit event arrived 120 minutes ago with `patient_location=ED` and no bed assignment has been made
**When** the boarding timer monitor runs (every 5 minutes)
**Then** a boarding alert notification is published to `notification-requests` with `priority=HIGH`, `patient_id`, `ed_arrival_time`, `minutes_elapsed=120`, `target_unit`.

### Scenario 2: Alert does NOT fire if bed assignment made before 2 hours
**Given** a patient arrived in the ED 90 minutes ago and was assigned to bed `3A-02` at 60 minutes
**When** the boarding monitor runs
**Then** no alert is fired for that patient (bed assignment confirmed before threshold).

### Scenario 3: Alert resolved when bed assignment confirmed
**Given** a boarding alert is active for patient in ED
**When** the bed manager assigns the patient to a bed via `PATCH /api/v1/beds/{id}/status` (marking as RESERVED)
**Then** the boarding alert `status` is updated to `RESOLVED`; no further alerts fire for that patient's current ED stay.

### Scenario 4: Alert idempotent — fires once per boarding event
**Given** a boarding alert was already sent for encounter `ENC-001`
**When** the monitor runs again 5 minutes later (patient still unassigned)
**Then** no duplicate alert is published (idempotency key: `boarding:{encounter_id}:{boarding_start_time}`).

---

## Definition of Done

- [ ] `BoardingMonitor` checks encounters with `current_location=ED` and no `bed_assignment` more than 120 minutes old
- [ ] Alert published to `notification-requests` with boarding-specific payload
- [ ] `boarding_alert_sent_at` field on `encounter` for idempotency
- [ ] Alert resolution on bed assignment event (set `boarding_alert_resolved_at`)
- [ ] Boarding monitor runs every 5 minutes (APScheduler)
- [ ] Unit tests: threshold detection, no-alert before threshold, idempotency, resolution
- [ ] Code reviewed and approved

---

## Technical Notes

- `current_location` field on `encounter`: set from PV1-3 (assigned patient location) in HL7 ADT parsing
- ED location codes: configurable list in `config/ed_locations.yaml` (facility-specific ADT codes)
- Boarding duration: `now - encounter.admit_time` (for A01) or `now - encounter.transfer_time` (for ED-originating transfer)
- Alert delivery SLA: boarding alerts must have `priority=IMMEDIATE` in Pub/Sub `notification-requests`
