# US-040: Activate High-Risk Care Pathway for Score ≥0.7

> **Epic:** EP-007 — Follow-up Care Agent & Risk Scoring | **Sprint:** 2 | **Points:** 3 | **Priority:** Must Have
> **Status:** Draft | **Date:** 2026-07-14

---

## User Story

**As a** care manager,
**I want to** be immediately alerted when a discharged patient has a risk score ≥0.7 so that I can arrange mandatory follow-up within 7 days, as required by hospital policy,
**So that** high-risk patients do not fall through the gaps after discharge, preventing a preventable readmission.

---

## Business Value

BR-003 (mandatory 7-day follow-up for high-risk patients) is a hospital policy with direct patient safety implications. Without automated pathway activation, high-risk patients identified by the model would not receive escalated follow-up unless a care manager manually reviewed every discharge risk score — an operationally impractical process at scale.

---

## Acceptance Criteria

### Scenario 1: HIGH risk tier triggers care manager alert
**Given** a patient's risk score is 0.75 (`risk_tier=HIGH`)
**When** the follow-up care agent processes the A03 event
**Then** a `CARE_MANAGER_ALERT` is published to `notification-requests` within 60 seconds; the alert contains `encounter_id`, `risk_score=0.75`, `risk_tier=HIGH`, `required_followup_days=7`.

### Scenario 2: 7-day follow-up record created in DB
**Given** a HIGH-risk patient is discharged
**When** the care pathway is activated
**Then** an `appointment` record is created with `appointment_type=HIGH_RISK_FOLLOW_UP`, `target_date = discharge_date + 7 days`, `status=SCHEDULED`, and a care manager `assigned_user_id`.

### Scenario 3: MEDIUM risk creates standard 14-day follow-up
**Given** a patient's risk score is 0.50 (`risk_tier=MEDIUM`)
**When** the follow-up care agent processes the A03
**Then** an appointment record is created with `target_date = discharge_date + 14 days`, `appointment_type=STANDARD_FOLLOW_UP`.

### Scenario 4: LOW risk creates 30-day follow-up only
**Given** a patient's risk score is 0.20 (`risk_tier=LOW`)
**When** the follow-up care agent processes the A03
**Then** an appointment record is created with `target_date = discharge_date + 30 days`, `appointment_type=ROUTINE_FOLLOW_UP`; no care manager alert is fired.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] `FollowUpCareAgent.process()` activates care pathway after risk score calculation
- [ ] Care manager alert: `POST` to `notification-requests` Pub/Sub for HIGH tier only
- [ ] Appointment record creation for all 3 tiers (different `target_date` and `type`)
- [ ] `appointment` ORM table: `encounter_id`, `appointment_type`, `target_date`, `status`, `assigned_user_id`
- [ ] Risk tier-to-pathway mapping in `config/care_pathways.yaml` (configurable follow-up days)
- [ ] Unit tests: HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW tier pathway logic, appointment creation, alert firing condition
- [ ] Code reviewed and approved

---

## Technical Notes

- Care manager assignment: route to `app_user` with `role=CARE_MANAGER` assigned to the patient's unit (round-robin or explicit assignment)
- Appointment `status` lifecycle: `SCHEDULED → CONFIRMED → COMPLETED | MISSED`
- Phase 1: appointment is an internal SmartHandoff record only; FHIR write-back deferred to Phase 2
