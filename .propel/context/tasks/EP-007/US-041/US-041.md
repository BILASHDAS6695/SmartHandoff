# US-041: Schedule 48-Hour Post-Discharge Check-In for Risk ≥0.5

> **Epic:** EP-007 — Follow-up Care Agent & Risk Scoring | **Sprint:** 2 | **Points:** 3 | **Priority:** Should Have
> **Status:** Draft | **Date:** 2026-07-14

---

## User Story

**As a** patient with a MEDIUM or HIGH readmission risk,
**I want to** receive a check-in message 48 hours after my discharge asking how I am doing and if I have any concerns,
**So that** early warning signs of deterioration are caught before they become a full readmission event.

---

## Business Value

48-hour post-discharge check-ins are one of the highest-ROI interventions for readmission prevention. Early detection of medication side effects or wound complications at 48 hours — rather than at re-presentation to the ED — prevents costly readmissions while improving patient experience (BO-04).

---

## Acceptance Criteria

### Scenario 1: 48-hour check-in scheduled for risk ≥0.5
**Given** a patient is discharged with `risk_score=0.6` (`risk_tier=MEDIUM`)
**When** the follow-up care agent processes the A03 event
**Then** a `SCHEDULED_NOTIFICATION` record is created with `type=CHECK_IN_48H`, `send_at = discharge_time + 48 hours`, `patient_phone` or `patient_email`.

### Scenario 2: Check-in not scheduled for LOW risk
**Given** a patient is discharged with `risk_score=0.2` (`risk_tier=LOW`)
**When** the follow-up care agent processes the A03
**Then** NO 48-hour check-in notification is scheduled (check-in only for risk ≥0.5).

### Scenario 3: Check-in delivered via patient's preferred channel
**Given** a patient has `preferred_contact=email` and `email=pat@example.com`
**When** the notification service delivers the 48-hour check-in
**Then** the email is sent to `pat@example.com` using the "48-hour check-in" SendGrid template; `delivery_status` updated to `SENT`.

### Scenario 4: Check-in respects opt-out flag
**Given** a patient has `notification_opt_out=True`
**When** the 48-hour check-in send time arrives
**Then** the notification service skips delivery and records `delivery_status=OPTED_OUT`; no SMS or email is sent.

---

## Definition of Done

- [ ] `ScheduledNotification` ORM model: `type`, `send_at`, `patient_id`, `encounter_id`, `channel`, `delivery_status`
- [ ] Follow-up care agent creates `CHECK_IN_48H` record for `risk_score ≥ 0.5`
- [ ] Notification service reads scheduled notifications from DB and dispatches at `send_at` time
- [ ] Opt-out flag respected: `patient.notification_opt_out=True` → skip + log
- [ ] Unit tests: schedule creation for correct risk thresholds, opt-out enforcement
- [ ] Code reviewed and approved

---

## Technical Notes

- `send_at` computed from `encounter.discharge_time + timedelta(hours=48)` — not from current time
- Notification service polling: every 5 minutes check for `send_at <= now AND delivery_status=PENDING`
- Check-in message content: "Hi [first_name], it's been 48 hours since your discharge. How are you feeling? Reply to let us know or call [care_team_number] with any concerns."
- PHI minimisation: use `patient.first_name` only in the message (not full name or MRN)
