# US-042: Escalate Post-Discharge Urgent Concerns Within 15 Minutes

> **Epic:** EP-007 — Follow-up Care Agent & Risk Scoring | **Sprint:** 2 | **Points:** 3 | **Priority:** Must Have
> **Status:** Draft | **Date:** 2026-07-14

---

## User Story

**As a** discharged patient experiencing chest pain,
**I want to** have my urgent concern flagged by the chatbot and escalated to the care team within 15 minutes,
**So that** I receive a response from a clinician quickly enough to prevent a medical emergency from worsening.

---

## Business Value

Post-discharge urgent symptom escalation is a patient safety feature. A patient experiencing a myocardial infarction symptom at home who flags it in the chatbot needs a clinician callback within 15 minutes (FR-053). This is not a nice-to-have — it is a direct life-safety requirement.

---

## Acceptance Criteria

### Scenario 1: Urgency flag from chatbot triggers escalation within 15 minutes
**Given** the patient chatbot (EP-008) has set `urgency_flag=True` for encounter `ENC-001`
**When** the follow-up care agent detects the urgency flag
**Then** a `CARE_TEAM_ESCALATION` notification is published to `notification-requests` within 60 seconds of the flag being set; the on-call nurse is notified via SMS.

### Scenario 2: Care team nurse acknowledges escalation within 15 minutes
**Given** an escalation alert was sent to the on-call nurse at T+0
**When** the nurse acknowledges via `PATCH /api/v1/care/escalations/{id}/acknowledge`
**Then** the escalation `status=ACKNOWLEDGED`, `acknowledged_at` is recorded; no further escalation reminders are sent.

### Scenario 3: Unacknowledged escalation re-escalates to supervisor after 15 minutes
**Given** a `CARE_TEAM_ESCALATION` alert was sent 15 minutes ago with no acknowledgement
**When** the escalation monitor runs
**Then** a `SUPERVISOR_ESCALATION` is published to `notification-requests`; the original alert is tagged `escalated_to_supervisor=True`.

### Scenario 4: Escalation acknowledgement API RBAC
**Given** a patient attempts to call `PATCH /api/v1/care/escalations/{id}/acknowledge` with a patient JWT
**When** the API is called
**Then** the response is `403 Forbidden`; only staff JWT holders (nurse, physician, charge_nurse roles) may acknowledge escalations.

---

## Definition of Done

- [ ] `CareEscalationMonitor` watches for `chatbot_transcript.urgency_flag=True` events (via DB poll or Pub/Sub)
- [ ] Escalation notification published within 60 seconds of urgency flag detection
- [ ] `PATCH /api/v1/care/escalations/{id}/acknowledge` endpoint with staff RBAC
- [ ] Unacknowledged escalation re-escalation at 15-minute mark (APScheduler)
- [ ] Unit tests: escalation trigger, acknowledgement, re-escalation, RBAC enforcement
- [ ] Code reviewed and approved

---

## Technical Notes

- Urgency flag detected via Pub/Sub: chatbot agent (EP-008) publishes `URGENCY_FLAG_SET` event to `patient-events` topic; follow-up care agent subscribes
- Escalation SLA is 15 minutes from flag detection to acknowledgement (not from chatbot message to acknowledgement)
- On-call nurse determination: lookup `app_user` with `role=ON_CALL_NURSE AND unit=encounter.current_unit`
- Emergency instructions must also be displayed to the patient immediately in the chatbot (handled by EP-008)
