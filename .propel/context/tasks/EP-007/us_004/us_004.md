# US-004: Escalate Post-Discharge Patient Concerns to Care Team Within 15 Minutes

> **Epic:** EP-007 — Follow-up Care Agent & Risk Scoring | **Sprint:** 2 | **Points:** 3 | **Priority:** Must Have

## User Story

**As a** floor nurse,
**I want** post-discharge patient concerns flagged by the chatbot escalated to me automatically within 15 minutes,
**so that** patients who develop complications after discharge get prompt attention before their condition worsens.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] **AC-1:** Patient Communication Agent (EP-008) publishes `PATIENT_CONCERN_ESCALATION` event to Pub/Sub when chatbot detects a clinical concern (not full urgency — those escalate immediately in EP-008 US-002)
- [ ] **AC-2:** Follow-up Care Agent subscribes to concern escalation events; within 15 minutes: notifies assigned care team nurse via SMS + SignalR dashboard alert; notification includes: patient name (encrypted for nurse viewing), concern summary, portal conversation link
- [ ] **AC-3:** Escalation acknowledged via `PATCH /api/v1/chat/escalation/{id}/acknowledge` by the nurse; unacknowledged escalations after 30 minutes trigger supervisor notification (BR-014)
- [ ] **AC-4:** `ChatbotTranscript.escalated=true` and `escalated_to_user_id` set on acknowledgement
- [ ] **AC-5:** Integration test: chatbot message "I'm having trouble breathing" (urgency-level) vs "I'm feeling dizzy" (concern-level) → verify urgency goes to EP-008 US-002 path, concern goes to this 15-minute escalation path

## Definition of Done

- [ ] 15-minute escalation SLA verified in integration test
- [ ] Urgency vs concern routing correctly distinguished in 10 test scenarios

## Dependencies

- EP-008 US-003 (Chatbot escalation routing)
- EP-013 US-001 (SMS notification to nurse)
- EP-003 US-002 (Unacknowledged escalation supervisor notification at 30 minutes)
