# US-044: Detect Urgency Signals and Display Emergency Contact Immediately

> **Epic:** EP-008 — Patient Communication Agent & Chatbot | **Sprint:** 2 | **Points:** 5 | **Priority:** Must Have
> **Status:** Draft | **Date:** 2026-07-14

---

## User Story

**As a** patient experiencing chest pain at home,
**I want to** have the chatbot immediately recognise my urgent symptom and display emergency contact information and a care team alert within 10 seconds,
**So that** I get directed to appropriate emergency care immediately without waiting for a chatbot response.

---

## Business Value

Urgency detection is a patient safety life-critical feature. A patient experiencing a cardiac event who types "chest pain" in the chatbot needs emergency guidance in seconds, not after the LLM considers its response. Fast pattern matching before LLM invocation ensures zero latency to emergency guidance display.

---

## Acceptance Criteria

### Scenario 1: "Chest pain" triggers urgency response within 10 seconds
**Given** a patient sends the message "I have chest pain and can't breathe"
**When** the urgency detector processes the message
**Then** within 10 seconds: (a) emergency contact information is displayed prominently in the chat UI, (b) a `CARE_TEAM_URGENCY_ALERT` is published to `notification-requests`, (c) `chatbot_transcript.urgency_flag=True` is persisted.

### Scenario 2: Multiple urgency keywords are detected
**Given** the urgency keyword list includes: "chest pain", "can't breathe", "severe bleeding", "unconscious", "stroke", "suicide"
**When** any of these phrases appear in a patient message
**Then** urgency is detected; the chatbot immediately shows: "⚠ Emergency Alert: This sounds serious. Call 911 immediately or go to the nearest emergency room. Your care team has been notified."

### Scenario 3: Semantic urgency detection supplements keyword matching
**Given** a patient sends "my heart is racing really fast and I feel dizzy" (no exact keyword match)
**When** semantic urgency scoring runs
**Then** the message scores above the 0.8 urgency threshold; urgency response is triggered (same as Scenario 1).

### Scenario 4: Non-urgent questions do not trigger emergency response
**Given** a patient sends "when should I take my metformin?"
**When** urgency detection runs
**Then** urgency is NOT triggered; the question proceeds to the normal chatbot Q&A pipeline (US-043).

---

## Definition of Done

- [ ] `UrgencyDetector` class: phase 1 keyword pattern matching, phase 2 Gemini semantic classification
- [ ] Urgency keyword list in `config/urgency_keywords.yaml` (configurable)
- [ ] Semantic urgency threshold: 0.8 on Gemini classification score (using `gemini-1.5-flash` with structured output `{urgency: bool, confidence: float}`)
- [ ] Emergency response: hardcoded immediate display (NOT dependent on LLM response)
- [ ] `chatbot_transcript.urgency_flag=True` set on urgency detection
- [ ] `CARE_TEAM_URGENCY_ALERT` published to `notification-requests` within 10 seconds
- [ ] Urgency detection runs BEFORE LLM call — not as post-processing
- [ ] Unit tests: keyword matches, semantic threshold, non-urgent exclusion
- [ ] Code reviewed and approved

---

## Technical Notes

- Two-phase detection: (1) fast regex keyword match (O(n) string scan, <10ms); (2) semantic Gemini classification only if keyword match fails (adds ~500ms)
- Emergency contact: configured per hospital in `config/emergency_contacts.yaml`
- Urgency alert payload: `{encounter_id, patient_first_name (only), urgency_message_summary, timestamp}` — minimum PHI
