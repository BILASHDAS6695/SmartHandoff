# US-002: Detect Medical Urgency Signals and Display Emergency Contacts Immediately

> **Epic:** EP-008 — Patient Communication Agent & Chatbot | **Sprint:** 2 | **Points:** 5 | **Priority:** Must Have

## User Story

**As a** patient experiencing a medical emergency,
**I want** the chatbot to recognise my emergency and show me how to get help immediately,
**so that** I don't waste time typing in a chatbot when I need to call 911.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] **AC-1:** Urgency signal detector runs on every incoming patient message BEFORE the Gemini API call; uses two-tier detection: (a) keyword matching (chest pain, can't breathe, can't breathe, severe bleeding, unconscious, stroke, heart attack, choking) and (b) Gemini-based semantic classification if keywords not matched
- [ ] **AC-2:** Urgency detected → immediate response (within 1 second, no Gemini streaming): "This sounds like a medical emergency. Please call **911** immediately or go to your nearest emergency room. We are also alerting your care team." — along with hospital ER number and 24-hour nurse line
- [ ] **AC-3:** Urgency alert published to `notification-requests` as `HIGH_PRIORITY_CLINICAL_ALERT`; care team nurse receives SignalR push AND SMS within 60 seconds
- [ ] **AC-4:** `ChatbotTranscript.urgency_flag=true` set for the triggering message; escalation tracked in `alert_log`
- [ ] **AC-5:** UI: urgency response displayed with a red banner, large font, and prominent "Call 911" button (tel: link); regular chatbot input disabled until patient dismisses the banner
- [ ] **AC-6:** Integration test: 15 urgency test phrases (chest pain, difficulty breathing, severe pain, etc.) all trigger urgency path; 15 non-urgency phrases (I feel tired, my wound looks red) do NOT trigger urgency path

## Definition of Done

- [ ] 15/15 urgency phrases detected correctly (zero false negatives on reference set)
- [ ] Emergency display visible in patient portal within 1 second in staging
- [ ] Care team alert confirmed received via SMS in end-to-end test
