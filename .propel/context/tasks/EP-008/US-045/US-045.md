# US-045: Route Care Team Escalation and Track Acknowledgement

> **Epic:** EP-008 — Patient Communication Agent & Chatbot | **Sprint:** 2 | **Points:** 3 | **Priority:** Must Have
> **Status:** Draft | **Date:** 2026-07-14

---

## User Story

**As a** patient who has requested help via the chatbot,
**I want to** know that my escalation has been received and that a nurse will contact me within 2 minutes,
**So that** I am reassured that my concern is being taken seriously and I know what to expect next.

---

## Business Value

The 2-minute escalation-to-nurse-contact SLA (FR-062) is what distinguishes SmartHandoff from a simple FAQ chatbot. Patients who receive confirmation that a real person has been notified are significantly less likely to call 911 for non-emergency concerns, freeing emergency resources for true emergencies.

---

## Acceptance Criteria

### Scenario 1: Patient receives "Help is on the way" confirmation
**Given** a care team escalation has been published to `notification-requests`
**When** the notification service delivers the SMS/alert to the on-call nurse
**Then** the chatbot displays "Your care team has been notified and will contact you within 2 minutes. If this is life-threatening, call 911 immediately." — displayed immediately after urgency detection, not after nurse acknowledgement.

### Scenario 2: Escalation delivery confirmed within 2 minutes
**Given** the escalation notification was published at T+0
**When** the on-call nurse acknowledges via the care team dashboard alert
**Then** acknowledgement is recorded at T+N; if N > 2 minutes, the encounter is flagged for response time review; no additional automatic escalation (escalation handled by EP-007 US-042).

### Scenario 3: Escalation record linked to chatbot transcript
**Given** an urgency message triggered a care team escalation
**When** `GET /api/v1/chat/escalations?encounter_id={id}` is called
**Then** the response includes the escalation record with `transcript_message_id`, `urgency_message`, `notified_user_id`, `acknowledged_at`, and `acknowledgement_time_minutes`.

### Scenario 4: Escalation API endpoint is patient-scoped read-only
**Given** a patient JWT holder calls `GET /api/v1/chat/escalations?encounter_id={id}`
**When** the request is made
**Then** only escalations for the patient's own `encounter_id` (matching JWT claim) are returned; the patient cannot view other patients' escalations.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] `ChatbotEscalation` ORM model: `encounter_id`, `transcript_message_id`, `notified_user_id`, `notified_at`, `acknowledged_at`, `channel`
- [ ] `POST /api/v1/chat/escalate` endpoint: creates escalation record + publishes to Pub/Sub
- [ ] `PATCH /api/v1/chat/escalation/{id}/acknowledge` endpoint (staff-only RBAC)
- [ ] `GET /api/v1/chat/escalations` endpoint: patient-scoped (own encounter only) + staff (all)
- [ ] Escalation acknowledgement time monitored: if >2 min, flag for review (log metric only in Phase 1)
- [ ] Unit tests: escalation creation, acknowledgement, patient scope enforcement
- [ ] Code reviewed and approved

---

## Technical Notes

- "Help is on the way" message is pushed to the patient's chat UI via a special `{type: ESCALATION_CONFIRMED}` message type — not a regular chatbot response
- `notified_user_id`: on-call nurse resolved from `app_user` table (same lookup as US-042)
- Do NOT block the chat response on escalation delivery — fire-and-forget to Pub/Sub; UI message shown immediately
